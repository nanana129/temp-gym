Excellent Points World-Class Level

1. Clear Progressive Overload

  • SQ: 30kg → 62.5kg (+108%)
  • DL: 70kg → 75kg
  • BP: 32kg → 37.5kg

This is the most fundamental principle in Strength & Conditioning research, and the top priority for both the NSCA and ACE. Your trainer executed this flawlessly.

2. Brilliant Rep Range Variation

  • Strength zone (1-6 reps): 37%
  • Hypertrophy zone (7-10 reps): 43%
  • Endurance zone (11-15 reps): 18%

This closely matches the "rep range continuum" advocated by Brad Schoenfeld. The approach resembles Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP), one of the most evidence-backed periodization methods.

3. Compound Ratio of 59% is Ideal

At 59%, your program exceeds what most gym trainers typically program (40-50%) and nearly matches the evidence-based ideal (60-70%).

4. 14% Functional Component = "Athlete-Type" Program

Box Jumps, Kettlebell Swings, Thrusters, Medicine Ball work — this aligns with the programming philosophy of Mike Boyle (NBA/NFL consultant) and Eric Cressey (MLB trainer). Your trainer integrated Concurrent Training — the most appropriate method for team sport athletes.

5. Focused Exercise Selection Per Session

An average of 2.1 exercises per session with 6-9 sets each aligns with Greg Nuckols' "fewer exercises, higher volume, higher concentration" approach.

⚠️ Push:Pull Ratio = 60:1

Evidence-based trainers worldwide agree that push and pull should maintain at least a 1:1 balance. This imbalance may cause forward posture, shoulder impingement risk, and bench press plateaus.

2. Periodization is "Intuitive" Rather Than Systematic

No intentional deload weeks are visible. However, the trainer may have been adjusting based on condition and feedback — a form of autoregulated periodization.

3. Upper Body Biased Toward Horizontal Plane

Horizontal pulls (rows): virtually zero. Vertical pulls (chin-ups/pulldowns): zero. Eric Cressey recommends 1:1 balance for both horizontal and vertical push/pull.
